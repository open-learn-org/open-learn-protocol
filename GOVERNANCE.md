# Governance

Open Learn Protocol is maintained by a small group of editors. The intent is to keep the bar low for adoption and the bar high for changes.

## Roles

- **Editors** merge PRs and shepherd RFCs to acceptance or rejection. Listed in [`MAINTAINERS.md`](./MAINTAINERS.md) (to be created when more than one editor exists).
- **Contributors** propose changes via issues, PRs, and RFCs. Anyone can be a contributor.
- **Implementers** are the audience the specs are written for. They are not obliged to participate in governance, but their feedback weighs heavily.

## Decisions

- **Editorial changes**: one editor approval, merge.
- **Substantive changes (RFCs)**: rough consensus among editors after a minimum two-week public comment window. If editors disagree, the change is declined. Forks are welcome.
- **New specs**: same process as RFCs but with a longer comment window (four weeks) and an explicit "this is now part of OLP" merge announcement.

## Conflicts of interest

Editors who work for an organization that implements a spec they're editing should disclose it. Disclosure is not disqualification; we expect editors to be implementers. Pretending otherwise produces worse specs.
